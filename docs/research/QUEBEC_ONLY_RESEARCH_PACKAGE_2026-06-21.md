# Quebec-Only ZONNING Research Package

Date: 2026-06-21
Status: research implementation, docs only
Guardrail: no code, UI, database, or deployment changes

## Core Question

How can ZONNING beat broad tender platforms inside Quebec without pretending to be bigger than MERX, SEAO, or SOVRA?

## Executive Answer

ZONNING should not position itself as another tender portal. SEAO owns official Quebec public tender publication, MERX/SOVRA is stronger for tender aggregation, document ordering, electronic submission, plan-holder visibility, alerts, and historical bid tools, and Ontopical/SOVRA is the serious pre-RFP intelligence competitor.

The strongest honest wedge is:

> Quebec construction intelligence before and around the official tender: permits, SEAO opportunities, RBQ/AMP/RENA fit, municipal signals, property/site context, and practical go/no-go guidance for Quebec contractors and suppliers.

That means ZONNING wins by helping a Quebec business answer:

1. Is this opportunity real and relevant to my trade, territory, licenses, and capacity?
2. Did something happen before SEAO/MERX that creates a sales or bid opportunity?
3. What compliance, site, municipal, or buyer context changes my chase/no-chase decision?
4. What should I do next this week?

ZONNING should be smaller than MERX and SEAO, but sharper for Quebec.

## Current ZONNING Truth To Anchor Claims

As of the latest local/live product check used for this research package, ZONNING has a meaningful Quebec data base but must not claim comprehensive coverage.

Current strengths:

- Public health endpoint is live.
- Public coverage API reports 30 datasets and 62 registered sources.
- Indexed layers include permits, tenders, RBQ licenses, zoning, transactions, assessment, heritage, contracts, contamination, roadworks, companies, and awards.
- Stronger indexed municipal permit coverage exists for Montreal and Quebec City.
- Existing tender layer reports hundreds of open tenders.

Current claim risks:

- Only a small number of municipalities are truly searchable at strong depth.
- Many cities are registered or document-only, not deeply indexed.
- Some useful layers depend on public source freshness and source availability.
- ZONNING should not claim to replace SEAO, MERX, Biddingo, ConstructConnect, or procurement submission workflows.

Recommended current claim:

> ZONNING is building a Quebec construction intelligence layer that connects permits, public tenders, company/license signals, and municipal/property context so Quebec teams can qualify opportunities faster.

Recommended forbidden claim:

> ZONNING is the complete Quebec tender platform or a replacement for SEAO/MERX.

### Where Quebec Opportunities Appear

- SEAO: official Quebec public tendering system and documents.
- Municipal websites and direct procurement pages.
- Donnees Quebec and municipal open-data portals.
- MERX/SOVRA and other aggregators.
- Biddingo and bids&tenders-style procurement networks.
- ConstructConnect and construction lead/bid platforms.
- Council agendas, minutes, budget documents, PTI/capital plans.
- Association and plan-room ecosystems such as ACQ/BSDQ-related workflows.
- Permit datasets and permit lists published by municipalities.

### Buying Triggers

- Tender deadline pressure.
- Missed early leads before public tender release.
- Uncertainty about whether the company is eligible or licensed for the work.
- RBQ, AMP, RENA, Revenu Quebec, insurance, bonding, or document compliance risk.
- Low bid win rate and wasted estimating time.
- Manual monitoring fatigue across many municipal and tender portals.
- Need to see who else is active around a project, buyer, site, or trade.
- Need for French/Quebec-specific source trust.

### Segment Urgency Ranking

1. Specialty subcontractors: high pain, concrete territory/trade fit, often relationship-driven.
2. Material suppliers: strong value if permit-to-lead and project context are actionable.
3. GCs/estimators: strong tender workflow pain, but many already use entrenched tools.
4. Developer/site scouts: high value from property/site layers, but not always tender-focused.
5. Public-sector bidders: compliance pain is high, but they may expect official-source workflows.

## Customer Jobs Report

### Job 1: Find Real Opportunities Without Checking 10 Places

Users currently monitor SEAO, MERX, municipal pages, association notices, emails, construction networks, LinkedIn, relationships, and sometimes local plan rooms. The repeated pain is not only "finding tenders"; it is deciding whether each notice is worth time.

### Job 2: Know If We Can Actually Chase This

Common checks before chase:

- Trade/scope fit.
- Geography and crew capacity.
- Deadline and mandatory visit.
- Bonding, insurance, and document requirements.
- RBQ license fit and restrictions.
- AMP authorization / RENA / Revenu Quebec requirements.
- Buyer history and incumbent risk.
- Plan-holder, bidders, or likely competitors.
- Project stage and timeline.

### Job 3: Get Early Signals Before The Tender

Early signals that may matter:

- Building permits.
- Council agenda items.
- Capital plans/PTI.
- SQI projections and public infrastructure forecasts.
- Roadwork programs.
- Zoning or site constraint changes.
- Developer activity.
- Assessment/transaction changes.

### Job 4: Turn Intelligence Into Action

ZONNING output must create a concrete next action:

- Call this owner/developer/buyer.
- Watch this project until a public tender appears.
- Prepare compliance documents before the deadline.
- Do not chase because license/territory/scope does not fit.
- Send to estimator with clear source links and documents.

## Personas To Validate

### Small Trade Owner

Pain: no time to monitor portals, wants jobs that match license, geography, crew, and relationships.

Winning promise: "Only show me Quebec opportunities I can actually chase this week."

Risk: if ZONNING feels like another feed, they will not pay.

### Estimator / BD Coordinator

Pain: too many tenders, too many documents, unclear fit, missed addenda, repetitive searches.

Winning promise: "Qualify faster before wasting estimating hours."

Risk: MERX/ConstructConnect workflows are already familiar.

### Supplier Sales Rep

Pain: public tenders come too late; wants project/permit signals earlier to sell materials.

Winning promise: "Find Quebec projects before the buying decision is locked."

Risk: permit-derived leads must include enough project context to act.

### Developer / Site Scout

Pain: site discovery requires many disconnected property, zoning, assessment, environmental, and municipal layers.

Winning promise: "One Quebec site context view before deeper due diligence."

Risk: may pull product away from contractor revenue wedge.

### Public-Contract Bidder

Pain: compliance, forms, documents, thresholds, RENA/AMP/Revenu Quebec/RBQ requirements.

Winning promise: "Avoid wasting time on bids you cannot or should not chase."

Risk: official submission and document purchase still belongs elsewhere.

## Competitor Conclusions

The detailed matrix is in `docs/research/COMPETITOR_MATRIX.md`. The first-10-minute UX teardown protocol is in `docs/research/UX_WORKFLOW_SCORECARD.md`.

### What Competitors Own

- SEAO owns official Quebec public tender trust and publication.
- MERX/SOVRA owns serious tender aggregation, documents, e-bid, plan-holder, alerts, and historical bid workflow.
- Ontopical/SOVRA owns serious pre-RFP municipal intelligence positioning.
- ConstructConnect owns construction-specific bid board and project workflow.
- Biddingo and bids&tenders own procurement network workflows for many public buyers.
- ProcureData owns normalized procurement data/API positioning.
- Local municipal portals own source-level freshness for specific cities.

### What They Leave Open

ZONNING can exploit gaps where broad platforms do not operationalize Quebec-specific context:

- Permit-to-lead tied to tenders and companies.
- RBQ license fit and restricted-license checks as opportunity qualification.
- AMP/RENA/Revenu Quebec compliance reminders as bid/no-bid intelligence.
- Municipal agenda, budget, PTI, roadwork, and permit signals packaged into a contractor workflow.
- Property/site intelligence tied to opportunity search.
- Honest Quebec coverage labels showing source, freshness, indexed depth, and confidence.
- French/Quebec-specific buyer language and source trust.

### ZONNING Should Not Compete Here

- Bid submission.
- Official tender document ownership.
- Broad Canadian tender aggregation as the primary promise.
- Every industry, every province, every public buyer.
- Generic RFP writing automation without Quebec procurement fit.
- "Complete data" claims that cannot be proven.

## UX Research Summary

The UX benchmark should score each competitor by the first 10 minutes of a Quebec contractor workflow:

1. Time to first relevant Quebec opportunity.
2. Search clarity.
3. Filter quality.
4. French/Quebec fit.
5. Trust and source visibility.
6. Document workflow.
7. Saved searches and alerts.
8. Bid/no-bid support.
9. Team collaboration.
10. Mobile usability.

### Best UX Patterns To Steal Ethically

- MERX: serious document/order/submit workflow and plan-holder context.
- Ontopical: early-signal context, local-government monitoring, and outreach workflow.
- ConstructConnect: construction-specific filters, bid board, project status, and team workspace.
- SEAO: official source trust, source-of-record clarity, and procurement seriousness.
- ProcureData: normalized data/API framing and searchable procurement document extraction.

### ZONNING UX North Star

The first screen should not be "search tenders." It should be:

> These are the Quebec opportunities and early signals worth your attention this week, with why they fit or do not fit.

## Validation Research

The detailed protocol is in `docs/research/VALIDATION_RESEARCH_PLAN.md`.

### Assumptions To Test

1. Quebec contractors do not need another tender portal; they need better go/no-go intelligence.
2. Permit-to-lead is useful enough to pay for.
3. RBQ/AMP/RENA fit creates trust and saves time.
4. Pre-RFP municipal signals are valuable only if filtered and actionable.
5. The first paying segment is more likely specialty subcontractors or suppliers than broad public-sector bidders.

### Required Validation

- 25 customer interviews for broad discovery.
- 15 focused validation interviews.
- 10 live workflow observations.
- 5 paid-intent tests.
- 5 landing-page/message tests.
- 5 manual concierge lead deliveries.

### Go Criteria

- At least 8 of 15 customers describe the problem as painful and current tools as incomplete.
- At least 5 of 15 agree to test with real company data.
- At least 3 of 15 show payment intent or agree to a pilot price.
- At least 5 users say ZONNING found or qualified something they would not have found quickly in SEAO/MERX.

### Kill Or Pivot Criteria

- Users only want cheaper MERX/SEAO.
- Users do not trust permit-derived leads.
- Users cannot explain what action they would take from ZONNING intelligence.
- Users say the output is interesting but not worth paying for.

## Reddit And LinkedIn Listening

The evidence table is in `docs/research/SOCIAL_LISTENING_EVIDENCE.md`.

### What Social Listening Already Suggests

Reddit is useful for complaint discovery, not statistical proof. LinkedIn is stronger for professional language, ecosystem mapping, associations, and buyer/practitioner narratives.

Early themes:

- Public procurement is seen as real but time-consuming and procedural.
- Some small businesses doubt whether tender portals are worth paying for.
- Users care whether an opportunity is small/large enough, real, and winnable.
- Construction platforms are criticized for coverage gaps, stale plans, cost, and poor scope fit.
- Local plan rooms and source-specific portals can beat broad tools on addenda, plan holders, and bid results.
- Relationship context matters; a lead alone is not enough.
- Quebec professional posts show demand for public-tender education, projections of upcoming tenders, RBQ/BSDQ/ACQ support, and better visibility.

## Positioning Recommendation

### Primary Positioning

> ZONNING helps Quebec construction teams find and qualify opportunities before they waste estimating time, by connecting permits, public tenders, RBQ/AMP/RENA fit, and municipal/site intelligence.

### Narrow Wedge

Start with Quebec specialty subcontractors and suppliers who sell into construction projects and public/private work.

Why:

- They feel opportunity-monitoring pain.
- They need early signals before tender release.
- They can act on permits and project context.
- They care about RBQ/compliance fit but do not want to become procurement experts.
- They are underserved by generic tender feeds.

### First Paid Offer To Test

"Quebec Opportunity Qualification Pilot"

Manual or semi-manual weekly delivery:

- 10 to 25 Quebec opportunities or early signals per week.
- Each includes source links, location, buyer/owner/project context, trade fit, RBQ/compliance notes, deadline/stage, and recommended next action.
- Customer provides trade, license class, territories, ideal job size, excluded work, and preferred buyers.

Pilot pricing to test:

- $199/month for small trade/supplier pilot.
- $499/month for estimator/BD team pilot.
- Higher only after users prove recurring value.
