# Quebec ZONNING Attack Research Dossier

Date: 2026-06-22
Status: success-grade research upgrade
Scope: research and strategy only, no code/product/deploy changes

## Brutal Diagnosis

The first research package was useful but too soft. It organized the work, but it did not answer the business question hard enough:

> Where can ZONNING win in Quebec soon, with a smaller product than MERX/SEAO, and with claims it can prove?

The answer is not "Quebec tender platform." That loses.

The answer is not "AI procurement search." That is generic.

The answer is not "all Quebec construction data." That is too broad and invites fake claims.

The best attack is:

> ZONNING should become the Quebec go/no-go intelligence layer for small and mid-market construction suppliers and specialty contractors, starting with public/municipal infrastructure and commercial permit-driven work.

## Current Strategic Answer

ZONNING wins only if it helps a Quebec company make a faster, better weekly decision:

1. Which opportunities should I chase?
2. Which should I ignore?
3. Which early signals should I watch before they become a tender?
4. What Quebec-specific compliance, license, source, site, or buyer risk matters?
5. What do I do next?

ZONNING should be a decision product, not a listing product.

## Why Quebec Is Still Worth Attacking

### 1. Public Infrastructure Spend Is Big And Current

The latest Quebec Infrastructure Plan 2026-2036 is not just "large"; it is an active demand signal. The plan totals 167.0 billion dollars over ten years. It emphasizes maintaining existing public assets, with 105.8 billion dollars, or 71 percent of planned investments, dedicated to maintenance of the public infrastructure portfolio.

That matters because maintenance work creates recurring, geographically distributed opportunities for trades, suppliers, subs, estimators, and BD teams.

Most important categories for ZONNING:

- Health and social services: hospitals, renovations, equipment-adjacent work, maintenance.
- Education: schools, maintenance, expansions, replacements.
- Roads and transport infrastructure.
- Public transit.
- Municipal water and infrastructure programs.
- Social/community housing.

Source: Quebec PQI 2026-2036.

### 2. Construction Activity Is Large Enough To Support A Niche

Quebec construction employs hundreds of thousands of people and represents a meaningful share of the provincial economy. Job Bank reports 315,700 construction workers across Quebec for 2022-2024, or 7.0 percent of average employment. CCQ forecasts more than 200,000 workers annually in regulated construction up to 2029 and roughly 211 million annual hours over the next several years.

This means the market is not tiny. But it also means teams are capacity-constrained. A product that reduces wasted estimating and chase time can be more valuable than a product that merely shows more tenders.

Sources: Job Bank Quebec construction profile; CCQ 2025-2029 outlook.

### 3. Procurement Information Is Public But Not Necessarily Useful

The opportunity is not that tenders are hidden. The opportunity is that public procurement information is fragmented, not always timely enough for suppliers, and often not directly useful for planning current or upcoming business opportunities.

The Office of the Procurement Ombudsman found that procurement information can meet reporting requirements while still having limited utility for suppliers trying to plan business opportunities. This supports ZONNING's wedge: usefulness, actionability, and planning.

Source: Office of the Procurement Ombudsman study on usefulness of procurement information.

### 4. Quebec Compliance Is A Real Differentiator

Quebec has a stronger integrity/compliance context than a generic tender feed captures:

- SEAO is the official path for public tenders.
- AMP authorization thresholds matter, including 5 million dollars for construction/public-private partnership contracts or subcontracts and 1 million dollars for services.
- RENA must be checked by public bodies and by businesses before public subcontracts.
- RBQ license status matters for construction work.
- Revenu Quebec attestation rules matter for many public and construction contracts.

This gives ZONNING a Quebec-specific decision layer that broad platforms do not naturally own.

Sources: AMP thresholds; AMP RENA; RBQ; Quebec public integrity rules.

### 5. Permit Data Is A Real But Messy Lead Source

Permits are valuable because they are early. Institut de la statistique du Quebec describes building permits as an early indicator for construction because permit issuance is one of the first steps of the construction process.

Montreal makes this commercially obvious: most boroughs publish monthly construction permit lists, and lists can be purchased monthly or through annual subscriptions. That means people already treat permit lists as useful enough to transact around, but the workflow is fragmented, borough-specific, and not automatically trade-qualified.

This is a real ZONNING wedge:

> Turn messy Quebec permit lists into qualified construction opportunities by trade, territory, project type, owner/site context, and next action.

Sources: Institut de la statistique du Quebec building permits; City of Montreal permit lists.

### Primary ICP

Small-to-mid Quebec specialty contractors and construction suppliers serving municipal/public infrastructure and commercial renovation work.

Examples:

- Excavation.
- Civil/site work.
- Paving/asphalt/concrete.
- Electrical.
- HVAC/mechanical.
- Plumbing.
- Roofing/envelope.
- Doors/windows/glazing.

- Traffic/road/safety suppliers.
- Water/sewer infrastructure suppliers.

### Why This Segment First

They have:

- Frequent opportunity-monitoring pain.
- High cost of chasing bad-fit work.
- Real need for local geography and source freshness.
- Enough revenue per won job to justify monthly spend.
- A practical use for permits, SEAO, roadwork plans, municipal agendas, and RBQ/compliance context.
- Less sophisticated internal procurement intelligence than large GCs.

They do not need:

- A full eBid replacement.
- National tender aggregation.
- A generic CRM.
- A dashboard full of vanity maps.

They need:

- "Show me the 12 things worth looking at this week and why."

## The Attack Wedge

### Product Category To Own

Quebec Opportunity Qualification.

Not tender search.
Not procurement automation.
Not construction CRM.
Not general lead gen.

### One-Sentence Positioning

ZONNING helps Quebec construction suppliers and specialty contractors decide what to chase this week by combining permits, SEAO/public opportunities, RBQ/AMP/RENA fit, municipal signals, and site context into qualified go/no-go briefs.

### Stronger Name For The Core Output

Quebec Qualified Opportunity Brief.

Every opportunity should answer:

- What happened?
- Where did it happen?
- What is the source?
- Is it a tender, permit, award, pre-RFP signal, roadwork plan, agenda item, or site/property change?
- Which trade/supplier profile does it match?
- Why is it worth attention?
- What could make it a bad chase?
- What Quebec compliance/license check matters?
- What is the deadline or timing signal?
- What is the recommended next action?

## Minimum Product That Could Actually Feel 10x

The smallest product is not a dashboard. It is a weekly decision workflow:

1. User sets company profile:
   - Trade/scope.
   - RBQ license/class.
   - Regions.
   - Job size.
   - Public/private preference.
   - Buyer types.
   - Exclusions.
   - Capacity.
2. ZONNING produces a weekly list:
   - 10 to 25 qualified opportunities/signals.
   - Each has source link, fit reason, risk reason, compliance note, and next action.
3. User marks:
   - Chase.
   - Watch.
   - Ignore.
   - Bad match.
4. ZONNING learns:
   - More like this.
   - Less like this.
   - Remove this buyer/source/trade.
5. Team gets digest:
   - This week.
   - Closing soon.
   - Early signals.
   - Compliance risks.
   - New permits in territory.

The dashboard can come later. The paid value is in the weekly qualified brief.

## Data Moat: The Real Moat Is The Join

No single data source is enough. MERX has tenders. SEAO is official. ConstructConnect has construction workflow. Ontopical has pre-RFP.

ZONNING's moat is the Quebec-specific join:

| Join | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| SEAO + RBQ | Is this construction opportunity relevant to this contractor's licensed scope? |
| SEAO + RENA/AMP/Revenu Quebec | Is there a public-contract eligibility/compliance issue? |
| Permit + company profile | Is this permit a real lead for this trade/supplier? |
| Permit + property/site layers | Is the site/project type worth action? |
| Municipal agenda/PTI + trade profile | Is a future project forming before tender release? |
| Roadwork/capital plan + suppliers/subs | Which infra suppliers/trades should prepare before the tender? |
| Awards + buyers + geography | Which buyers repeatedly buy relevant work? |
| User feedback + source signals | What is actually relevant to this company's territory and work? |

## Pricing Hypothesis

Competitor pricing makes a paid wedge plausible:

- ConstructConnect advertises $129/month per market for Starter and $199/month per market for Professional.
- MERX has a free registration/pay-as-you-go path and premium packages for broader/unlimited access.
- Biddingo advertises supplier registration/subscription patterns and access to bid packages where available.
- Montreal permit lists can be purchased monthly or by annual subscription in borough workflows.

This suggests a first ZONNING pilot should not be $29. It should test whether users will pay for qualified intelligence:

- $199/month: small trade/supplier pilot.
- $499/month: estimator/BD coordinator/team pilot.
- $1,000+/month: only if concierge lead delivery produces direct sales/bid pipeline value.

The price is justified only if the output saves estimating/sales time or identifies opportunities they would not have found quickly.

## Validation Plan That Can Actually Kill Bad Ideas

### Concierge Test

Do not build first. Manually produce the weekly qualified brief for 5 companies.

For each company:

1. Collect company profile.
2. Build 10 to 25 qualified Quebec opportunities/signals.
3. Include exact source links.
4. Include reason to chase and reason to ignore.
5. Include compliance/license/source/site note.
6. Review live with user.
7. Ask which they would act on this week.

### Success Standard

The wedge is real only if:

- 3 of 5 concierge users ask for next week's report.
- 2 of 5 take a real action from a ZONNING-supplied signal.
- 3 of 5 say at least one item was not quickly findable in their normal workflow.
- 2 of 5 accept a paid pilot.
- At least one user gives a direct "this saved me time" or "this is a lead" story.

### Failure Standard

Stop or pivot if:

- Users say the list is interesting but do nothing.
- Users only want raw tenders cheaper than MERX.
- Users do not trust permit leads.
- Users cannot map the intelligence to a call, bid, watch, or ignore action.
- The team cannot produce enough relevant weekly items for a narrow profile.

## Interview Questions That Matter Most

The old script is fine, but these questions are the ones that decide the company:

1. Show me the last job you chased and the tabs/tools you used.
2. What made you waste time on a bad opportunity?
3. What opportunity did you miss and how did you find out too late?
4. What would make you ignore a tender immediately?
5. Which Quebec-specific compliance/license checks slow you down?
6. Do permits ever tell you something early enough to act?
7. If I gave you 15 qualified Quebec signals this week, which ones would you call or bid on?
8. Would you pay $199/month to get this every week if it matched your trade and territory?
9. What proof would make you trust ZONNING more than another feed?
10. What result after 30 days would make you keep paying?

## Research Verdict

The market is not asking for "a better SEAO." It is asking for fewer bad chases, earlier useful signals, and Quebec-specific confidence.

The strongest honest wedge is:

> Quebec Qualified Opportunity Briefs for construction suppliers and specialty contractors.

The first product should feel like a knowledgeable Quebec BD/estimating assistant that reads the sources, filters the noise, and says:

> Chase this. Watch this. Ignore this. Here is why. Here is the source. Here is the Quebec-specific risk.

That is how ZONNING can be smaller than MERX and still more valuable to the right Quebec user.
