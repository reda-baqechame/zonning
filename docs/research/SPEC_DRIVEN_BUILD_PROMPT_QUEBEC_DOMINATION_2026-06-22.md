## Market Evidence To Preserve In Product Strategy

Quebec is worth attacking because:

- The latest Quebec Infrastructure Plan 2026-2036 totals 167.0 billion dollars over ten years.
- The plan emphasizes maintaining existing public assets, with 105.8 billion dollars, or 71 percent of planned investments, dedicated to maintenance of the public infrastructure portfolio.
- Maintenance work creates recurring, geographically distributed opportunities for trades, suppliers, subs, estimators, and BD teams.
- Most important categories for ZONNING: health and social services, education, roads and transport infrastructure, public transit, municipal water and infrastructure programs, social/community housing.
- Quebec construction employs hundreds of thousands of people and represents a meaningful share of the provincial economy.
- Job Bank reports 315,700 construction workers across Quebec for 2022-2024, or 7.0 percent of average employment.
- CCQ forecasts more than 200,000 workers annually in regulated construction up to 2029 and roughly 211 million annual hours over the next several years.
- Teams are capacity-constrained. A product that reduces wasted estimating and chase time can be more valuable than a product that merely shows more tenders.
- Procurement information is public but not necessarily useful.
- The opportunity is not that tenders are hidden. The opportunity is that public procurement information is fragmented, not always timely enough for suppliers, and often not directly useful for planning current or upcoming business opportunities.
- The Office of the Procurement Ombudsman found that procurement information can meet reporting requirements while still having limited utility for suppliers trying to plan business opportunities.
- Quebec compliance is a real differentiator.
- SEAO is the official path for public tenders.
- AMP authorization thresholds matter, including 5 million dollars for construction/public-private partnership contracts or subcontracts and 1 million dollars for services.
- RENA must be checked by public bodies and by businesses before public subcontracts.
- RBQ license status matters for construction work.
- Revenu Quebec attestation rules matter for many public and construction contracts.
- Permit data is a real but messy lead source.
- Permits are valuable because they are early.
- Institut de la statistique du Quebec describes building permits as an early indicator for construction because permit issuance is one of the first steps of the construction process.
- Montreal makes this commercially obvious: most boroughs publish monthly construction permit lists, and lists can be purchased monthly or through annual subscriptions.

Use this exact wedge:

> Turn messy Quebec permit lists into qualified construction opportunities by trade, territory, project type, owner/site context, and next action.

## Scoring Logic

Implement deterministic, inspectable scoring first. Do not hide behind opaque AI.

### Fit Score

Fit score should combine:

- Trade/scope match.
- Region/municipality match.
- Job size match.
- Timing/deadline match.
- Buyer preference match.
- Source trust/freshness.
- Compliance/license fit.
- Site/project context fit.

Expose the score components in the UI. Do not show only one magic number.

### Risk Score

Risk score should include:

- Deadline too close.
- Source stale.
- Document-only/placeholder coverage.
- Missing buyer/owner/project details.
- RBQ/license mismatch or unknown.
- RENA/AMP/Revenu Quebec warning.
- Job value outside target range.
- Region outside target.
- Trade/scope uncertain.
- Official document/submission required elsewhere.

### Confidence Score

Confidence score should represent how much source evidence ZONNING has.

High confidence:

- Source URL exists.
- Source authority is known.
- Freshness date exists.
- Trade/category is mapped.
- Region/municipality is known.
- Deadline/stage is known where relevant.
- Linked source records reinforce the opportunity.

Low confidence:

- Missing source URL.
- Placeholder/document-only coverage.
- Stale source.
- Unclear trade.
- No buyer/owner/project.
- Inferred relationship without evidence.

# RESEARCH APPENDIX FOR AGENT CONTEXT

The agent must also preserve and apply the following research content.

## Core Question

How can ZONNING beat broad tender platforms inside Quebec without pretending to be bigger than MERX, SEAO, or SOVRA?

## Executive Answer

ZONNING should not position itself as another tender portal. SEAO owns official Quebec public tender publication, MERX/SOVRA is stronger for tender aggregation, document ordering, electronic submission, plan-holder visibility, alerts, and historical bid tools, and Ontopical/SOVRA is the serious pre-RFP intelligence competitor.

The strongest honest wedge is:

> Quebec construction intelligence before and around the official tender: permits, SEAO opportunities, RBQ/AMP/RENA fit, municipal signals, property/site context, and practical go/no-go guidance for Quebec contractors and suppliers.

That means ZONNING wins by helping a Quebec business answer:

1. Is this opportunity real and relevant to my trade, territory, licenses, and capacity?
2. Did something happen before SEAO/MERX that creates a sales or bid opportunity?
3. What compliance, site, municipal, or buyer context changes my chase/no-chase decision?
4. What should I do next this week?

ZONNING should be smaller than MERX and SEAO, but sharper for Quebec.

## Customer Jobs Report

### Job 1: Find Real Opportunities Without Checking 10 Places

Users currently monitor SEAO, MERX, municipal pages, association notices, emails, construction networks, LinkedIn, relationships, and sometimes local plan rooms. The repeated pain is not only "finding tenders"; it is deciding whether each notice is worth time.

### Job 2: Know If We Can Actually Chase This

Common checks before chase:

- Trade/scope fit.
- Geography and crew capacity.
- Deadline and mandatory visit.
- Bonding, insurance, and document requirements.
- RBQ license fit and restrictions.
- AMP authorization / RENA / Revenu Quebec requirements.
- Buyer history and incumbent risk.
- Plan-holder, bidders, or likely competitors.
- Project stage and timeline.

### Job 3: Get Early Signals Before The Tender

Early signals that may matter:

- Building permits.
- Council agenda items.
- Capital plans/PTI.
- SQI projections and public infrastructure forecasts.
- Roadwork programs.
- Zoning or site constraint changes.
- Developer activity.
- Assessment/transaction changes.

### Job 4: Turn Intelligence Into Action

ZONNING output must create a concrete next action:

- Call this owner/developer/buyer.
- Watch this project until a public tender appears.
- Prepare compliance documents before the deadline.
- Do not chase because license/territory/scope does not fit.
- Send to estimator with clear source links and documents.

## Personas To Validate

### Small Trade Owner

Pain: no time to monitor portals, wants jobs that match license, geography, crew, and relationships.

Winning promise: "Only show me Quebec opportunities I can actually chase this week."

Risk: if ZONNING feels like another feed, they will not pay.

### Estimator / BD Coordinator

Pain: too many tenders, too many documents, unclear fit, missed addenda, repetitive searches.

Winning promise: "Qualify faster before wasting estimating hours."

Risk: MERX/ConstructConnect workflows are already familiar.

### Supplier Sales Rep

Pain: public tenders come too late; wants project/permit signals earlier to sell materials.

Winning promise: "Find Quebec projects before the buying decision is locked."

Risk: permit-derived leads must include enough project context to act.

### Developer / Site Scout

Pain: site discovery requires many disconnected property, zoning, assessment, environmental, and municipal layers.

Winning promise: "One Quebec site context view before deeper due diligence."

Risk: may pull product away from contractor revenue wedge.

### Public-Contract Bidder

Pain: compliance, forms, documents, thresholds, RENA/AMP/Revenu Quebec/RBQ requirements.

Winning promise: "Avoid wasting time on bids you cannot or should not chase."

Risk: official submission and document purchase still belongs elsewhere.
