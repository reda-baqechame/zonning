# Quebec Competitor Matrix

Date: 2026-06-21
Scope: Quebec-only ZONNING positioning research

## Summary

ZONNING should not compete as a full tender portal. The defensible opening is a Quebec opportunity intelligence layer that helps contractors and suppliers qualify work with permits, tenders, RBQ/AMP/RENA fit, municipal signals, and site context.

## Deep Teardown Notes

### SEAO

Strengths:

- Official Quebec public procurement source.
- Public bodies and municipalities publish calls and contract information.
- Official document access and e-bidding workflows.
- Open contracting data exists through SEAO/OCDS exports.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Official systems are not optimized for contractor go/no-go intelligence.
- Users still need to interpret trade fit, compliance fit, site context, and buyer/project history.
- Public tender release can be late relative to sales and preparation.

ZONNING strategy:

- Treat SEAO as source of truth.
- Add contractor fit, source links, compliance signals, permit/site context, and next action.

### MERX/SOVRA

Strengths:

- Mature supplier network.
- Large tender aggregation claim.
- Real-time notifications.
- Document download and electronic bid submission.
- Plan Holder List.
- Historical bid data.
- Broad public/private coverage.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Broad platform, not Quebec-construction-specific.
- Does not own municipal permits, property context, RBQ fit, or local site intelligence as the core wedge.
- Users may still waste time on opportunities that are technically available but not worth chasing.

ZONNING strategy:

- Never claim to beat MERX on documents, eBid, or broad tender network.
- Beat MERX on "is this Quebec opportunity worth my time and what should I do next?"

### Ontopical/SOVRA

Strengths:

- Strong "before the RFP" positioning.
- Monitors agendas, meeting minutes, planning discussions, and local-government signals.
- Claims 6-12 month early opportunity visibility.
- AI recommendations and outreach context.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Broad local-government intelligence, not necessarily a Quebec construction/site/compliance cockpit.
- Quebec-specific RBQ/AMP/RENA/permit/site combination can be more concrete for contractors.

ZONNING strategy:

- Do not copy generic pre-RFP intelligence.
- Build Quebec construction-specific early signals that connect directly to trade, territory, license, and next action.

### ConstructConnect

Strengths:

- Construction-specific project and bid board workflow.
- Filters by project stage, bid type, work type, category, funding, value, and bid date.
- Strong for estimators, GCs, and subcontractors.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Broad construction workflow, not Quebec civic/regulatory intelligence.
- May not combine permits, SEAO, RBQ/AMP/RENA, local council signals, and site constraints into a Quebec-first product.

ZONNING strategy:

- Respect ConstructConnect as a workflow benchmark.
- Compete on Quebec local context and sharper qualification, not broad bid-board maturity.

### Biddingo / bids&tenders

Strengths:

- Public-sector procurement networks.
- Supplier matching and alerts.
- Buyer-side eProcurement workflows.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Fragmentation remains when users must monitor several portals.
- Procurement-network products do not necessarily tell a Quebec contractor whether a job fits license, territory, crew, and compliance.

ZONNING strategy:

- Use as monitored opportunity sources when possible.
- Do not become an eProcurement network.

### GovBid / BidFit / BidFilter

Strengths:

- Simpler guidance and tender aggregation for Canadian SMBs.
- Explain that there is no single Canadian tender source.
- Useful for SMB education.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- Generic Canada-wide guidance is not enough for Quebec construction.
- Limited site/property/compliance intelligence.

ZONNING strategy:

- Own the Quebec construction vertical deeply.

### ProcureData

Strengths:

- Normalized procurement data.
- REST API and Snowflake.
- Open tenders from CanadaBuys/SEAO.
- Attached RFP PDF extraction.
- Pre-solicitation signals and contract/vendor lifecycle data.

Weaknesses ZONNING can exploit:

- More data-product/API oriented.
- Does not automatically become a contractor's weekly go/no-go workflow.

ZONNING strategy:

- Learn from normalized data model.
- Build a workflow and decision layer, not only an API.

## Pricing Research To Complete

Pricing is often gated, sales-led, or plan-dependent. The research process must capture:

- Public price pages when available.
- Trial limits.
- Per-seat vs company pricing.
- Document/order costs.
- Alert/search limitations.
- Whether Quebec-specific coverage is included or extra.
- Whether historical data, plan holders, or exports require higher tiers.

## UX Teardown Checklist

For each competitor, record the first 10 minutes as a Quebec contractor:

- Search: "Montreal electrical", "Laval excavation", "Quebec City HVAC", and one French query.
- Try to find one open Quebec opportunity.
- Try to find deadline, buyer, documents, plan holders, addenda, and source.
- Try to save search or set alert.
- Try to determine whether the opportunity is worth chasing.
- Score friction from 1 to 5.
