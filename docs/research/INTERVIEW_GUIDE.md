# Quebec ZONNING Interview Guide

Date: 2026-06-21
Purpose: customer discovery and validation for a Quebec-only construction intelligence wedge

## Interview Rule

Do not pitch first. Make the user show their real workflow before showing ZONNING concepts.

## Recruiting Targets

| Segment | Count | Examples |
|---|---:|---|
| Subcontractors | 8 | Electrical, HVAC, excavation, concrete, roofing, civil, plumbing, finishing |
| Material suppliers | 5 | Building materials, civil materials, mechanical/electrical suppliers |
| GCs/estimators | 5 | General contractors, estimator teams, bid coordinators |
| Developers/consultants | 4 | Site scouts, permit consultants, planning/real-estate consultants |
| Public-sector bid/procurement-adjacent experts | 3 | Procurement consultants, former municipal buyers, bid advisors |

## Screener

Ask:

1. Are you based in Quebec or selling into Quebec construction/public work?
2. Do you search for construction projects, tenders, permits, or public opportunities at least monthly?
3. Which tools or sources do you currently use?
4. Have you used SEAO, MERX, ConstructConnect, Biddingo, a municipal portal, or a local plan room?
5. Can you show a real recent workflow during the call?

Reject or deprioritize:

- No Quebec relevance.
- No role in finding, qualifying, bidding, or selling into opportunities.
- Only wants generic lead scraping with no source trust.

## Opening Script

"I am researching whether Quebec contractors and suppliers need better opportunity intelligence, not another tender portal. I am not here to sell you today. I want to understand how you find work, decide what to chase, and where current tools fail."

Ask permission:

"Can I take notes and quote anonymous phrases internally? I will not publish your name or company without permission."

## Workflow Questions

1. Walk me through the last opportunity you chased.
2. Where did you find it first?
3. What tools did you check?
4. What made it look relevant?
5. What almost made you reject it?
6. Who else on your team touched it?
7. How did you save, share, or track it?
8. What documents or source links did you need?
9. Did you have to check addenda, plan holders, bid results, or buyer history?
10. How confident were you before spending time on it?

## Time And Cost Questions

1. How many hours per week do you spend looking for opportunities?
2. How many hours per week do you spend deciding whether to chase?
3. How many bids or leads did you ignore last month?
4. How many did you chase but later regret?
5. What does one wasted estimate or sales chase cost you?
6. What does one missed good opportunity cost you?

## Tool Stack Questions

Ask about:

- SEAO.
- MERX/SOVRA.
- ConstructConnect.
- Biddingo.
- bids&tenders.
- Municipal portals.
- Donnees Quebec.
- Association plan rooms.
- Internal spreadsheets.
- CRM.
- Email alerts.
- LinkedIn/network referrals.

Questions:

1. Which one do you trust most?
2. Which one annoys you most?
3. Which one would be hardest to cancel?
4. What do you still do manually after using these tools?
5. Which alerts are useful and which are noise?

## Compliance And Fit Questions

1. What RBQ license or classes matter for your work?
2. Have license, bonding, insurance, AMP, RENA, Revenu Quebec, or other requirements ever stopped you from bidding?
3. How do you know if a job is legally or practically a fit?
4. Who checks compliance?
5. What mistakes cause rejection or wasted time?
6. Would a visible compliance/fit checklist change your chase decision?

## Permit-To-Lead Questions

1. Do building permits ever create sales or bid opportunities for you?
2. Which permit details would matter?
3. How soon after a permit is issued is it still useful?
4. Who would you contact from a permit signal?
5. What would make a permit lead feel untrustworthy?
6. Would you pay for permit-derived leads if they were filtered by trade and territory?

## Pre-RFP Municipal Signal Questions

1. Do you watch council agendas, minutes, budgets, capital plans, or roadwork plans?
2. If yes, how?
3. If no, why not?
4. What future-project signal would be early enough to matter?
5. What would make it too early or too vague?
6. What next action would you take from a pre-RFP signal?

## Paid Pilot Questions

After workflow discovery, test the pilot:

"If we delivered a weekly Quebec opportunity qualification report for your trade, territory, license, and job size, with source links and go/no-go reasons, would that be useful?"

Ask:

1. What would need to be in the report?
2. How many relevant opportunities per week would be enough?
3. What would make you trust it?
4. What would make you cancel?
5. Would you test this with your real criteria?
6. Would you pay $199/month for a small pilot?
7. Would your team pay $499/month if it saved estimating or sales time?
8. Who approves this purchase?

## Concept Test

Show three one-line concepts and ask which is clearest:

1. "Quebec construction intelligence before the tender."
2. "Permits + SEAO + RBQ fit in one weekly opportunity list."
3. "Stop wasting estimating time on bids you should not chase."

Ask:

- Which one makes sense?
- Which one sounds like fake marketing?
- What would you expect to see after clicking?
- What proof would you need?

## Segment-Specific Probes

### Subcontractor

- Which GCs or buyers do you prefer?
- Do you care who else is bidding?
- Do you use BSDQ workflows?
- What project size is too small or too large?

### Supplier

- How early do you need to know about a project?
- Do permits help you reach owners, GCs, or trades?
- What product categories map to permit/tender signals?

### GC / Estimator

- How do you organize bid invites?
- How do you handle addenda?
- How do you decide whether to bid?
- What historical buyer/bidder data helps?

### Developer / Consultant

- Which site constraints matter first?
- How do zoning, heritage, contamination, wetlands, flood, and assessment data change decisions?
- What municipality data is hardest to gather?

### Procurement-Adjacent Expert

- Where do bidders make mistakes?
- What official systems cannot solve?
- Which compliance requirements are misunderstood?
- What would be risky for ZONNING to claim?
